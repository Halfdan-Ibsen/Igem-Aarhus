Project Blueprint

RNA origami as a programmable engineering platform.

We structure the project into clear design stages so each iteration improves both folding reliability and functional output.

Problem Framing

Conventional biomolecular tools often separate structure from function, slowing prototyping and limiting modularity.

Current Gap

Many systems require multiple molecules or unstable assemblies, making behavior hard to predict and scale.

Our Response

We encode shape and activity in one RNA strand that self-folds and hosts functional motifs at defined locations.

Design-to-Learn Pipeline

A scannable engineering workflow with measurable checkpoints at every stage.

1. In Silico Design

Multi-objective sequence optimization for fold stability, motif accessibility, and predictable geometry.

2. Build

DNA template generation and expression construct preparation for robust RNA production.

3. Test

Fluorescence and gel-based characterization to validate structure, yield, and behavior.

4. Learn

Experimental feedback updates design parameters for stronger next-generation scaffold candidates.

Expected Outcomes

Deliver reproducible design rules and a reusable scaffold framework for future iGEM teams and broader RNA engineering applications.

Reliable

Demonstrated folding consistency across selected scaffold families.

Modular

Interchangeable motif placements with retained structural behavior.

Portable

A pipeline that can be reused for diagnostics, computing, and therapeutic concepts.
