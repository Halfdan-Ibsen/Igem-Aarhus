Project Description

Our RNA origami platform turns sequence design into programmable nanoscale geometry for real biological impact.

Background

Current biomolecular tools often force a tradeoff between structure and function. RNA origami resolves this by building both into one strand: it self-folds into stable shapes while carrying aptamers, binding loops, and reporter motifs.

Our Design Stack

  • In silico design: multi-objective sequence optimization for folding, accessibility, and motif placement.
  • Build: DNA templates for transcription and expression constructs for intracellular production.
  • Test: fluorescence and gel-based characterization to verify structural behavior.
  • Learn: model updates from experimental data to improve each generation.

Expected Outcomes

We aim to demonstrate reliable RNA origami folding with measurable functional output and clear design rules for portability. Success means future teams can rapidly repurpose our scaffold framework for diagnostics, molecular computing, and smart therapeutics.

Vision

Long-term, we want RNA origami to be a core synthetic biology design language—where biological function is not just encoded, but architected.
