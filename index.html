Future of programmable biomaterials

RNA Origami, but make it iconic.

We fold RNA into nanoscale architectures that sense biology, carry function, and unlock next-generation synthetic systems.

Project Abstract

Our project engineers RNA origami scaffolds that self-assemble inside cells and organize functional motifs with nanometer precision. By encoding fold logic directly in sequence design, we create modular RNA devices for biosensing, molecular imaging, and programmable therapeutic delivery.

Design Principle

Single-stranded RNA folds into predefined shapes through internal base pairing, giving us compact structures with precise docking sites.

Why It’s Cool

RNA origami combines information storage, structure, and dynamic function in one molecule—biology’s version of nanoscale engineering.

Goal This Season

Build a robust design-to-validation pipeline that links computational folding, wet-lab expression, and fast structural readout.
